3. Explain briefly how LCD and CRT technologies form screen images.

LCD (Liquid Crystal Display), LCD screens use liquid crystals sandwiched between two layers of glass or plastic. When electrical currents pass through the liquid crystals, they change alignment to block or allow light, which is then filtered through colored layers to create images.

CRT (Cathode Ray Tube), CRT screens use electron beams that are directed onto a phosphorescent screen. The electron beams hit different parts of the screen coated with phosphor, causing it to glow in different colors to form the image (Tyson, n.d.)

5. Explain how HDD and SSHD differ internally.

HDD (Hard Disk Drive), HDDs use spinning magnetic disks (platters) and read/write heads that move across the disk to access data. Data is stored magnetically on the platters, and the mechanical movement of the read/write heads makes HDDs relatively slow compared to SSDs.

SSHD (Solid State Hybrid Drive), SSHDs combine a traditional HDD with a small amount of solid-state storage (SSD). The SSD component caches frequently accessed data for faster access, while the HDD provides larger storage capacity. This hybrid approach offers a balance between speed and storage capacity.

(Bramt, 2024).

6. Differentiate between DAS, NAS, SAN, and cloud storage.

DAS (Direct Attached Storage), Storage that is directly connected to a single computer, such as an external hard drive. It is not shared over a network.

NAS (Network Attached Storage), A storage device connected to a network that allows multiple users and devices to access and share files over the network.

SAN (Storage Area Network), A high-speed network that provides access to consolidated block-level storage, typically used in enterprise environments to connect servers to storage devices.

Cloud Storage, Online storage service that allows data to be stored on remote servers accessible via the internet, providing scalability and remote access.

(Castagna, 2024).
